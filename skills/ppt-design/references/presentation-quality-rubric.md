# Presentation Quality Rubric

Use this rubric when the deck is meant for `client-facing` or `public-stage` delivery.

## Categories

| Category | Weight | What Good Looks Like |
|---|---:|---|
| Narrative Clarity | 20 | Each slide has one clear takeaway and fits the overall story arc. |
| Style / Scenario Fit | 15 | The chosen style supports the delivery context instead of fighting it. |
| Brand Fidelity | 15 | Brand mark, footer, palette discipline, and voice stay consistent. |
| Visual Hierarchy | 15 | One dominant focal point, strong headline/body separation, obvious reading order. |
| Readability | 15 | Projection-safe contrast, distance-safe type size, no low-contrast microcopy. |
| Data / Diagram Clarity | 10 | Metrics, comparisons, and structured figures remain legible and truthful. |
| Cross-Slide Pacing | 5 | Layout variety exists, but the deck still feels coherent. |
| Delivery Readiness | 5 | Speaker notes and presenter-safe framing are present when required. |

## Public-Stage Pass Rule

- Total score must be `>= 85 / 100`.
- No category may fall below `60%` of its own weight.
- Any hard fail blocks release even if the total score passes.

## Hard Fails

- Projection contrast is below threshold.
- More than two focal points compete on one slide.
- Headline/body hierarchy is too weak for stage viewing.
- The chosen style is not approved for the current presentation scenario.
- Required brand lockups are missing.
- Dark-theme projector safety threshold is not met.
